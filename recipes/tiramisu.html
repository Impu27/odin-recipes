Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert known for its creamy, rich, and indulgent flavor. It consists of layers of coffee-soaked ladyfinger biscuits (savoiardi) and a luscious mascarpone cheese mixture, often flavored with cocoa powder and sometimes a hint of liqueur like Marsala or rum. The dessert is typically chilled to allow the flavors to meld together, creating a soft and velvety texture.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfinger biscuits - 250g
  • Espresso coffee - 350ml
  • Marsala wine - 3tbsp
  • Mascarpone cheese - 500g
  • Egg yolks - 4 large
  • Granulated sugar - 100g
  • Heavy cream - 120ml
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder - 2 tbsp

Steps

  1. Prepare the Coffee Base: Brew espresso, let it cool, and mix with optional liqueur (Marsala, rum, or coffee liqueur).
  2. Make the Cream Mixture: Whisk egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy. Add mascarpone cheese and mix until smooth. If using, gently fold in whipped cream for a lighter texture.
  3. Assemble the Layers: Quickly dip ladyfingers into the coffee mixture (do not soak too long to avoid sogginess). Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in a dish.
  4. Add Cream: Spread half of the mascarpone mixture evenly over the ladyfingers.
  5. Repeat Layers: Add another layer of coffee-dipped ladyfingers and top with the remaining mascarpone mixture.
  6. Dust and Chill: Dust the top with cocoa powder and refrigerate for at least 4 to 6 hours (or overnight) to allow flavors to meld.
