Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich, comforting layers of pasta, sauce, cheese, and often meat. Typically made with wide, flat sheets of pasta, the dish alternates between layers of marinara sauce, a creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese mixture, and a meat filling, such as ground beef or sausage, although vegetarian variations can use spinach or other vegetables. Once assembled, the lasagna is baked until golden and bubbly, with the flavors melding together into a savory and satisfying meal. It's a versatile dish, allowing for many variations depending on regional preferences or personal tastes, but it remains a beloved comfort food worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles - 15 sheets
  • Ground beef - 500g
  • Onion - 1 medium
  • Garlic - 3 cloves
  • Marinara sauce - 500g
  • Ricotta cheese - 2 cups
  • Mozzarella cheese - 2 cups
  • Parmesan cheese - 1/2 cup
  • Basil
  • Olive oil - 2 tbsp
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the lasagna noodles: If using regular lasagna noodles, cook them according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare meat sauce: Cook ground beef, onion, garlic, and marinara sauce. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. Make cheese mixture: Mix ricotta, egg, Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
  4. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Assemble lasagna: Layer sauce, noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella, and repeat.
  6. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes, then uncover and bake for 10-15 more minutes.
  7. Let rest: Cool for 10 minutes before slicing.
